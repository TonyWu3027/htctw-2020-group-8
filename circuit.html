Electrical Design

One of the project objectives is to monitor the seawater quality to help restore the lagoon ecosystem. The hardware is comprised of sensors, signal conditioning circuit, microcontroller, and communication system to measure the proxy indicators and to send that to the software system for further data processing. The sensors deployed will monitor dissolved oxygen content, pH, oil content, salinity, and the greenness of water (a proxy indicator for presence of algae). 

High-Level Hardware Block Diagram 

Different sensors share a similar working principle in our design. The block diagram below is an overview of the hardware.  

In general, the sensor detects physical properties and converts them to electrical signals. This process is known as signal conditioning. Then, the signal is amplified and is passed through a low pass filter to eliminate the high frequency noises. Finally, it goes into an analog-to-digital convertor (ADC) to convert signal into digital form that could be read by microcontroller. The programmed microcontroller processes the received signals and sends the reading of each parameter to the software system to monitor the water and ecosystem. 

Sensor Circuit Design: pH Sensor Circuit as an Example

The pH probe circuit tests the acidity of seawater based on the concentration of hydrogen ions.  

The pH electrode has different voltages that across its surface due to different acidity in the water. In contrast, the reference electrodes provide a voltage baseline. The difference in voltages between the electrodes, obtained by passing the signals to the signal conditioning circuit, measures the pH values in proxy.  

The electrodes are connected to a “buffer”, which prevents signals or voltage being affected by other circuit, improving stability and accuracy of the measurements.  

The low-pass filter helps to remove high frequency signals (noise), hence the signal will be smoother. 

The analog to digital converter (ADC) converts the electrical signal into digital signal that can be read by the microcontroller. The non-inverting amplifier ensures the electrical signal fits the range of signal that the ADC can process, to have a digital signal with a higher resolution for microcontroller to give an accurate reading.  

The working principal of the other sensors is similar to the aforementioned.
