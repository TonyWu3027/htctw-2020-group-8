Our interdisciplinary engineering solution for monitoring the marine ecosystem of Lake Bardawil in Sinai, Egypt.

Overview

Over time, the depth of Bardawil lagoon in Sinai, Egypt has decreased from 40m to 1.5 m [1] and 80-100 dead sea turtles [2] have been documented. Human activities such as overfishing, pollution and methods of fishing have severely disrupted the marine ecosystem of the lagoon.  

Our goal is to design a device to monitor and obtain understandable data e.g seawater quality and species biodiversity, to help the fishermen and local management communities in maintaining a healthy marine ecosystem. 

Technical Summary

An anchor holds the device in place (see Monitoring Device Design). The solar panel powers the device, excess energy is stored in a lithium-ion battery, which powers the device during the night. The probes measure pH, temperature, dissolved oxygen, oil content, salinity, and greenness of the seawater in the lagoon. The real–time fisheye camera captures the ecosystem’s condition to monitor biodiversity. The microcontroller processes the electrical signals from the sensors and camera (see Electrical Design) and uploads data to the software system (see Software System Architecture) via the antenna. 

How the solution meets project objectives?

The device monitors all aspects of the marine ecosystem (temperature, pH, amount of dissolved oxygen and oil, salinity, greenness of the seawater, and biodiversity).  The integrated machine learning algorithm predicts the trends in lagoon conditions which helps in assessing the ecosystem’s health. This can prevent the adverse effects caused by human activities, such as overfishing. By helping locals realise the consequences caused by human activities, hence we can aim to provide local fisherman with a sustainable income. 

Feasibility Considering the Risks and Constraints

Economic

There is a risk that the government may unable to solely finance the project. However, we believe the project is financially feasible as the total cost of 40 devices is around £18,400 which is relatively small compared to the Egyptian government budgeted SDG funds ($9.8 billion for SDG 2030 finances [5]) and we could receive investment from other investors.  

Technical and Environmental

  • Manufacturing process of the materials used such as fibre reinforced polymers (FRP) have low carbon emissions and can be reused.
  • FRP does not degrade which would not pollute the sea. It also confines the inner content hence any poisonous leaks from the battery will not go into the sea, the aforementioned mitigate any risk relating to the device harming the environment. 
  • The architecture of the system software is readily available since it is designed on AWS. 
  • There is an ethical risk that pertaining to data being stolen and utilised for illegal purposes however, AWS provides robust security and a strong privacy data protection structure. 
  • Solar power is used which acts as a greener alternative power source. 

Schedule

The fishing season lasts from May to December, thus there are 4 months to deploy the device without conflicting with fishing activities. The time period is sufficient since a small number of devices will be deployed first for testing and increase with time. 

Legal

The project involves cooperation with the Egyptian government to restore the lagoon. As it contributes towards the UNDP’s Sustainable Development Goals of reserving marine ecosystems, it is very likely to be permitted by the government.
