Monitoring Device Design

System Needs

In order to be compatible with the other parts of our design and the theme of the whole project, the hardware platform is expected to meet the following needs:

  • Wireless communication and data transfer ability 
  • Waterproof
  • Corrosion resistant (inert/ no reaction with salinity, pH, dissolved oxygen, nitrate contents) 
  • High strength (Withstanding the turbidity of currents and potential knocks) 
  • Must not be big in size (does not obstruct the ecosystem and fishing activities) 
  • Sample testing should ideally be conducted in a continuous time period and data is transferred to the software systems (i.e. samples do not need to be sent to the labs)

Key Components Explanations:

Components

         Details 
Microcontroller control centre  
  • Consist of electrical circuits with control systems which processes the electrical signal from the sensors (signal amplification and noise filtering) and sends the data to the computers or cloud systems through the antenna 
Probe sensors [1][2] (Secondary transducer: physical properties converted to electrical signals) 
  • Temperature: platinum resistive sensor (stable, linear output, chemically inert and resistant to contamination 
  • pH: combination of electrodes to produce potential difference with Wheatstone bridge configuration for temperature compensation 
  • Dissolved oxygen: Galvanic cell which produces voltage output proportional to the concentration of dissolved oxygen 
  • Salinity:  same as pH electrodes which measures conductivity of the sea 
  • Greenness (pigment concentration) and oil (pollution): Intensity of reflected UV fluorescence is measured to determine the concentration of algae and oil content
Floating platform (casing) 
  • Fibre reinforced polymers used as casing material: light, tough, strong, high fatigue strength, very high melting point, corrosion resistant and easily manufacturable 
Solar panel 
  • Renewable power source and self-cleaning glass surface via rain fall 
Realtime fisheye camera 
  • Continuously captures the condition of the ecosystem in order to monitor biodiversity.  
  • Fisheye lenses correct the distortion of images due to the different refractive index in the sea.  
  • Object detection algorithms (e.g. OpenCV) integrated to account for the numbers of each species.  
Anchor 
  • Concrete core with vinyl plastic casing [3]
